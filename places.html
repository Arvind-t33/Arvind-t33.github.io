CERN

The European Organization for Nuclear Research operates the largest particle physics laboratory in the world. Located in a northwest suburb of Geneva on the Franco-Swiss border, it serves as a hub of bleeding-edge particle physics research... One of the main attractions (for interested tourists and physicists) is the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), the largest and highest-energy particle collider in the world, best known for its discovery of the Higgs boson.

Dusseldorf

With the 6th largest living standard in the world and a culturally driven, yet modern, lifestyle, Dusseldorf is not only an amazing tourist attraction, but a desirable place to reside in. Being one of the economic powerhouses of... Germany, it's constantly expanding IT sector, coupled with an affordable living cost makes this an excellent place of work. The affordable living cost is a byproduct of the efficient German health system.

The Alps

Excellent but volatile weather in the region tends to attract tourists who wish to experience the Alps even for short durations. With over 1800 glaciers and vast pastures with diverse flora and fauna, beauty here is found on an unprecedented scale... The Alps are divided into 6 regions based on their location: Swiss, Austrian, French, Italian, Bavarian and Julian Alps; each of which have their own form of beauty and culture.

Yosemite National Park

With granite rocks that glow like fire, species of plants and animals found nowhere else and one of the tallest waterfalls in the world, Yosemite National Park is a no-brainer addition to most people's bucket lists... The national park, first signed as one in the Yosemite Land Grant,once bid to host the winter Olympics. 75% of its visitors come to the park between May through October.

Serengeti National Park

Being an area with one of the most diverse animal populations in the world ,the Senrengeti National Park offers views of animals living their daily lives: simple infrequent occurences that we find spectacular. The Serengeti is home to one of the largest... concentrations of predators in the world. Being an important spot for human evolution, the area is also home to indegenous tribes like the Maasai people who still live preserve their rich culture inspite of western influence.

Ladakh

Ladakh is high above the sea: up to 25,400 feet high to be precise. This, in itself is an attraction to adventure-driven tourists from all around the world. What makes this place all the more surreal is that fact that it is also the location to 2 major lakes, the Pangong... and Tso Kar lake, a 108 feet tall statue of Buddha in an aesthetic location some 10,000ft above the sea and surprisingly, sand dunes. The Hunder Sand Dunes are home to the rare double humped Bactrian camels that have evolved to adapt to the extreme cold, drought and high altitudes.
